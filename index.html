🍇 LO1 — Vineyard & Environment
Grape Anatomy›
Skin: Colour pigment, tannins, aromatic compounds
Pulp: Sugar, acidity, water — colourless
Seeds: Harsh bitter tannins — avoid crushing

Skin thickness → tannins:
Thick skin = more tannins + deeper colour. Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, Malbec.
Thin skin = fewer tannins + pale colour. Pinot Noir, Grenache, Nebbiolo.

Berry size → concentration:
Small berries = high skin-to-juice ratio = more tannin + flavour per litre.
Large berries = more dilute, lower tannins.
Cabernet Sauvignon: small berry + thick skin = very high tannins.
Merlot: larger berry + thinner skin = softer, rounder tannins.

💡 Pulp is colourless → Blanc de Noirs Champagne (white wine from red grapes)
💡 Tannins come from skin, seeds and stems — NOT from pulp
Climate Effects›

Environmental Factors›
Altitude: +100m = -0.6°C → preserves acidity (Mendoza)
Rivers: Reflect light, moderate temp, create mist
Slate (Schiefer): Absorbs + releases heat — Mosel
Limestone: Drainage + mineral character
Concentration Methods — Sweet Wines›
Noble Rot: Sweet Cool humid mornings + warm dry afternoons. Sauternes, Tokaji, BA/TBA.

Raisining: Sweet Dried after harvest. Amarone.

Icewine/Eiswein: Very Sweet Frozen grapes pressed. Canada, Germany.
European Labelling›
France: AOC/AOP → IGP → Vin de France
Italy: DOCG → DOC → IGT
Spain: DOCa (Rioja+Priorat only) → DO → VdlT

🍶 LO2 — Winemaking & Ageing
Production by Style›
Red: Dry Crush → macerate skins → ferment → press → age
White: Usually Dry Crush → press immediately → ferment cool
Rosé: Usually Dry Short skin maceration (hours)
Sweet (quality): Sweet Stop fermentation early = residual sugar
Vessel Types›
Stainless Steel: Neutral, fresh, fruity
Concrete: Neutral, adds texture
New French Oak: Vanilla, spice, cedar — subtle
New American Oak: Vanilla, coconut, dill — bold
Old Oak: Oxygen only, no flavour
MLF & Lees Ageing›
MLF: Malic acid → Lactic acid. Softer, creamier, buttery.
Encourage: reds, oaked whites. Prevent: Riesling, Sauv Blanc.

Lees: Dead yeast → brioche, biscuit, creamy (autolytic).
Sur Lie = on the lees label term.
Bottle Ageing Changes›
Red: Purple → ruby → garnet → brick. Tannins soften. Leather, tobacco, earth (tertiary).

White: Pale → gold → amber. Honey, nuts, PETROL (Riesling).

Primary=grape. Secondary=winemaking. Tertiary=bottle age.
🍷 LO3 — Principal White Grapes
Chardonnay›
Dry Neutral — reflects climate and winemaking.

Chablis (cool): Green apple, chalk, mineral. Unoaked. High acidity.
Meursault/Burgundy (moderate): Peach, butter (MLF), vanilla (oak). Full body.
Napa/AUS (warm): Mango, pineapple, heavy oak. Full body, low acidity.
Pinot Grigio / Pinot Gris›
Italian Pinot Grigio: Dry Light, high acidity, lemon, apple. Unoaked. Simple.

Alsace Pinot Gris: Off-dry to Sweet Full body, honey, ginger, spice, complex.

💡 Same grape — completely opposite styles.
Sauvignon Blanc›
Dry High acidity, aromatic, almost never oaked.

Loire (Sancerre): Grapefruit, flint, mineral. Limestone. Elegant.
Marlborough NZ: Passion fruit, guava, tomato leaf. Explosive.
Bordeaux: Blended with Sémillon.
Riesling›
Dry to Very Sweet — full spectrum. Very high acidity. Never oaked.

Aged aroma: PETROL (TDN) ← KEY EXAM FACT
Mosel: Off-dry, delicate, slate. Alsace: Dry, full, powerful. Clare/Eden AUS: Bone dry, lime.
🍷 LO3 — Principal Red Grapes
Cabernet Sauvignon›
Dry Very high tannins. High acidity. Full body. Ages brilliantly.

Left Bank Bordeaux: Cassis, cedar, graphite. Pauillac. 10-20yr+
Napa Valley: Blackberry, chocolate, eucalyptus.
Chile/Coonawarra: Cassis, mint. Great value.
Merlot›
Dry Medium tannins. Medium acidity. More approachable young.

Right Bank Bordeaux: Plum, violet, velvety. Pomerol, Saint-Émilion.
Chile: Soft, plummy, great value.
Thinner skin than Cab Sauv → softer tannins.
Syrah / Shiraz›
Dry Same grape, different names and styles.

N.Rhône Syrah (cool): Pepper, violet, smoke. Elegant, structured. Hermitage.
Barossa Shiraz (warm): Blackberry jam, chocolate, licorice. Generous.
Pinot Noir›
Dry Low tannins. High acidity. Pale ruby. Climate sensitive.

Burgundy: Red cherry, earthy, elegant. Gevrey-Chambertin.
Oregon/Central Otago: Cherry, mineral, spice.
💡 Most versatile red — salmon, duck, mushrooms.

🌍 LO4 — Regional Varieties
🇫🇷 France
Grenache, Gamay, Viognier›
Grenache: Dry S.Rhône, Châteauneuf-du-Pape. Red fruit, high alcohol. GSM blend: +Syrah +Mourvèdre.

Gamay: Dry Beaujolais. Carbonic maceration. Strawberry, low tannin. Drink young.

Viognier: Always Dry Condrieu. Full body, LOW acidity. Apricot, peach, white flowers.
Chenin Blanc, Sémillon, Gewurztraminer, Furmint›
Chenin Blanc: Dry/Sweet/Sparkling Loire. Very high acidity = versatile. Savennières (dry), Vouvray (sweet), Crémant (sparkling).

Sémillon: Dry/Sweet Bordeaux blends + Sauternes (noble rot). Thin skin → Botrytis.

Gewurztraminer: Off-dry to Sweet Alsace. Lychee, rose, ginger, spice.

Furmint: Very Sweet Tokaj, Hungary. Tokaji Aszú. 3-6 puttonyos.
🇮🇹 Italy
Sangiovese & Nebbiolo›
Sangiovese: Dry Tuscany. High tannin + very high acidity. Sour cherry, leather.
Chianti Classico. Brunello DOCG (5yr). Rosso DOC (1yr) = Baby Brunello.

Nebbiolo: Dry Piedmont. VERY high tannin+acidity. PALE ruby. ROSES + TAR.
Barolo DOCG (3yr) = King. Barbaresco DOCG (2yr) = more elegant.
🇪🇸 Spain & 🌍 New World
Tempranillo, Albariño, Malbec, Pinotage›
Tempranillo: Dry Rioja DOCa, Ribera del Duero. Strawberry, leather. American oak = vanilla, coconut, dill.

Albariño: Dry Rías Baixas. Very high acidity. Citrus, peach, SALINE. Always unoaked.

Malbec: Dry Mendoza, Argentina. Dark fruit, chocolate, velvety. High altitude = good acidity.

Pinotage: Dry South Africa. Pinot Noir × Cinsault (1925). Smoky, earthy, dark fruit.
🍾 LO5 — Sparkling Wines
Sparkling Methods›
Traditional Method: 2nd fermentation IN BOTTLE → lees ageing → riddling → disgorgement → dosage. Complex, autolytic. Fine bubbles. Champagne, Cava, Crémant, Cap Classique.

Tank/Charmat: 2nd fermentation in TANK. Fresh, fruity, no autolytic. Prosecco (Glera grape).

Carbonation: CO2 injected. Cheap, coarse bubbles.
Champagne Key Facts›
Grapes: Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Meunier (+ Arbane, Petit Meslier, Pinot Blanc, Gris)
NV: Min 15mo (12 on lees). Vintage: Min 36mo. Exceptional years only.
Blanc de Blancs: 100% Chardonnay. Blanc de Noirs: 100% red grapes.
NM=buys grapes. RM=grower Champagne.

🇫🇷 France — Regions in Detail
Burgundy
Côte de Nuits — Red Burgundy Villages›
The northern half of the Côte d'Or — greatest Pinot Noir in the world.

Gevrey-Chambertin: Most powerful, structured Pinot Noir. Grand Crus: Chambertin, Chambertin Clos de Bèze
Morey-Saint-Denis: Between Gevrey and Chambolle. Grand Crus: Clos Saint-Denis, Clos de la Roche
Chambolle-Musigny: Most elegant, perfumed Pinot Noir. Grand Crus: Musigny, Bonnes-Mares
Vougeot: Clos de Vougeot — famous walled Grand Cru vineyard
Vosne-Romanée: Greatest Burgundy village. Grand Crus: Romanée-Conti, La Tâche, Richebourg
Nuits-Saint-Georges: No Grand Crus but excellent Premiers Crus. More tannic style
Côte de Beaune — White Burgundy Villages›
Southern Côte d'Or — greatest Chardonnay in the world.

Aloxe-Corton: Red and white. Grand Crus: Corton (red), Corton-Charlemagne (white)
Meursault: Greatest village for white Burgundy. No Grand Crus but famous Premiers Crus: Perrières, Charmes, Genevrières
Puligny-Montrachet: Grand Crus: Montrachet (shared with Chassagne), Chevalier-Montrachet, Bâtard-Montrachet
Chassagne-Montrachet: Also red Pinot Noir. Grand Crus shared with Puligny
Santenay: Southern Côte de Beaune. Good value Pinot Noir
Chablis — The Four Tiers›
100% Chardonnay. Kimmeridgian limestone. Always unoaked (Grand Cru sometimes oaked).

Petit Chablis: Lightest, simplest. Hillside vineyards away from Kimmeridgian soils
Chablis: Classic Chablis character. Green apple, lemon, chalk, high acidity
Chablis Premier Cru: 40 named vineyards. Better concentration and complexity. Montée de Tonnerre (best known)
Chablis Grand Cru: 7 Grand Cru vineyards on one south-facing slope. Les Clos (most structured), Valmur, Grenouilles, Vaudésir, Preuses, Bougros, Blanchot
Bordeaux — Left Bank Appellations›
Cabernet Sauvignon dominant. Gravelly soils.

Médoc: Large appellation covering northern left bank
Haut-Médoc: Higher quality sub-zone. Includes 6 communal appellations
Pauillac: Most prestigious left bank commune. Châteaux: Latour, Lafite Rothschild, Mouton Rothschild (3 of the 5 First Growths)
Margaux: Most elegant, perfumed left bank. Château Margaux (1st Growth)
Saint-Julien: Most consistent commune. Châteaux Léoville-Las-Cases, Ducru-Beaucaillou
Saint-Estèphe: Most tannic, structured. Cos d'Estournel, Montrose
Pessac-Léognan: South of Bordeaux. Haut-Brion (1st Growth). Also excellent dry whites (Sauvignon Blanc + Sémillon)
Bordeaux — Right Bank & Sweet Wine›
Merlot dominant. Clay soils.

Saint-Émilion: UNESCO heritage. Classified as Premier Grand Cru Classé. Château Pétrus-rival Cheval Blanc and Ausone at top
Pomerol: No official classification. Merlot on clay. Château Pétrus (100% Merlot, most expensive Bordeaux)
Sauternes: Noble rot (Botrytis) sweet wine. Sémillon + Sauvignon Blanc + Muscadelle. Villages: Sauternes, Barsac. Château d'Yquem = only Premier Cru Supérieur
Barsac: Can use Barsac or Sauternes label. Slightly lighter style
Rhône Valley
Northern Rhône — Syrah Country›
Côte-Rôtie: "Roasted slope." Two parts: Côte Brune (Syrah dominant) + Côte Blonde (Syrah + Viognier). Most elegant N.Rhône red
Condrieu: 100% Viognier. Dry, full body, low acidity, apricot/peach/white flowers. Very expensive
Château-Grillet: Single estate appellation. Viognier. One of France's smallest AOCs
Saint-Joseph: Large appellation. Syrah red + Marsanne/Roussanne white. More accessible prices
Crozes-Hermitage: Surrounds Hermitage. Much larger, more accessible, excellent value
Hermitage: The great hill. 100% Syrah (red) or Marsanne+Roussanne (white). One of France's greatest wines
Cornas: 100% Syrah, no white grapes permitted. Very powerful, structured, needs long ageing
Southern Rhône — Grenache Blends›
Châteauneuf-du-Pape: Up to 18 permitted varieties. Grenache dominant. Famous for galets (large rounded stones). Château Rayas, Château Beaucastel. Also white CdP (Grenache Blanc, Roussanne, Clairette)
Gigondas: Similar to CdP but more affordable. Grenache + Syrah + Mourvèdre
Vacqueyras: Step up from Côtes du Rhône. Grenache dominant
Rasteau: Red VDN (Grenache) + dry Grenache reds
Beaumes-de-Venise: Muscat de Beaumes-de-Venise (VDN, sweet, floral) + dry Grenache reds
Tavel: France's most famous rosé appellation. Grenache dominant. Dry, full-bodied
Loire Valley
Loire — Key Appellations by Grape›
Sauvignon Blanc: Sancerre (Kimmeridgian limestone, mineral/flinty) · Pouilly-Fumé (flint soils, gunflint note) · Menetou-Salon (more affordable, similar style) · Quincy · Reuilly

Chenin Blanc: Vouvray (dry to sweet, including sparkling) · Montlouis-sur-Loire (similar to Vouvray) · Savennières (dry, austere, ages 20yr+) · Quarts de Chaume Grand Cru (sweet noble rot) · Bonnezeaux (sweet noble rot) · Crémant de Loire (sparkling)

Cabernet Franc: Chinon (red, elegant, Loire's greatest red) · Bourgueil · Saint-Nicolas-de-Bourgueil · Saumur-Champigny

Muscadet: Melon de Bourgogne. Muscadet Sèvre et Maine (best subzone). Sur Lie ageing = autolytic texture
🇮🇹 Italy — Regions in Detail
Piedmont
Barolo DOCG — The King of Italian Wines›
100% Nebbiolo. Minimum 3 years ageing (5 years Riserva). The most complex, age-worthy Italian red.

Key villages (communes):
La Morra: Most aromatic, elegant style. Softer tannins. Sandy soils (Tortonian)
Barolo village: Powerful and structured. Mix of soil types
Castiglione Falletto: Very concentrated, structured. Mix of soils
Serralunga d'Alba: Most powerful, longest-lived. Helvetian (older, harder) soils
Verduno: Most delicate, floral style

Famous producers: Giacomo Conterno, Bruno Giacosa, Gaja (controversial Barberesco), Bartolo Mascarello, Roberto Voerzio
Tuscany — Chianti & Super Tuscans›
Chianti Classico DOCG: Historic zone between Florence and Siena. 80%+ Sangiovese. Black cockerel (Gallo Nero) symbol. Gran Selezione = top tier (single vineyard, 30mo). Villages: Greve in Chianti, Radda, Gaiole, Panzano

Brunello di Montalcino DOCG: 100% Sangiovese (Brunello clone). Village of Montalcino. 5yr min (6yr Riserva). Among Italy's most age-worthy reds. Biondi-Santi created it

Super Tuscans: IGT wines using non-traditional grapes (Cabernet, Merlot, Syrah). Exempt from DOC rules. Examples: Sassicaia (Cab Sauv/Cab Franc, Bolgheri), Tignanello (Sangiovese+Cab), Ornellaia

Other: Morellino di Scansano (coastal Sangiovese) · Vino Nobile di Montepulciano (Sangiovese/Prugnolo Gentile) · Vernaccia di San Gimignano (white, towers)
🇩🇪 Germany — Regions in Detail
Mosel — The Steep Slate Vineyards›
Riesling on steep south-facing slate (Schiefer) slopes. Cool. Lightest, most delicate German Rieslings.

Middle Mosel key villages:
Bernkastel: Bernkasteler Doctor — one of Germany's most famous vineyards
Wehlen: Wehlener Sonnenuhr — famous sundial vineyard. JJ Prüm (legendary producer)
Graach: Graacher Himmelreich
Piesport: Piesporter Goldtröpfchen — very famous though quality varies
Urzig: Ürziger Würzgarten — red sandstone soils, spicy character unusual for Mosel
Zeltingen, Neumagen-Dhron: Also excellent

Producers: JJ Prüm, Egon Müller, Moselland, Ernst Loosen
VDP Classification — Germany's Quality Pyramid›
The VDP (Verband Deutscher Prädikatsweingüter) is an association of top producers with their own classification:

Grosse Lage / Grosses Gewächs (GG): Top single vineyards, dry style, equivalent to Grand Cru
Erste Lage: Premier Cru equivalent
Ortswein: Village wine
Gutswein: Estate wine (entry level)

The VDP classification runs parallel to the official Prädikat system. A GG is always dry (trocken) — different from sweet Auslese or TBA which are in the Prädikat system.
🇦🇺 Australia / 🇳🇿 New Zealand — Detail
Barossa Valley — Old Vine Shiraz in Detail›
Warm continental climate. Many vines over 100 years old (pre-phylloxera). Some vines 150+ years.

Key producers: Penfolds (Grange = Australia's most famous wine), Henschke (Hill of Grace), Torbreck, Two Hands, Peter Lehmann

Barossa Grounds sub-regions: Marananga, Seppeltsfield, Nuriootpa, Angaston, Light Pass

Style: Rich, jammy, generous Shiraz. Blackberry, chocolate, licorice, sometimes vanilla (American oak traditional). High alcohol 14-15%. Opposite of elegant Northern Rhône Syrah

Also notable: Grenache (some oldest Grenache vines in world), Mourvèdre (old vine), Riesling (Eden Valley just adjacent)
Marlborough NZ — Sauvignon Blanc in Detail›
South Island. Free-draining stony soils. Intense UV radiation + cool nights = intense aromatics.

Sub-regions:
Wairau Valley: Main valley, river alluvial soils. Most production. Classic tropical/passion fruit style
Awatere Valley: Further south, cooler, more wind. More mineral, herbaceous, restrained style
Southern Valleys: Clay-rich soils. Fuller body

Key producers: Cloudy Bay (made Marlborough famous, 1985), Brancott Estate (first commercial Marlborough SB), Dog Point, Greywacke, Villa Maria

Grape varieties: Sauvignon Blanc dominant. Also Pinot Noir, Pinot Gris, Riesling, Chardonnay
